Samosa

Samosa is a fried or baked pastry with a savory filling, including ingredients such as spiced potatoes, onions, peas, and lentils. It originated in the Middle East and was brought to India where it became highly popular.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Potatoes
  • Peas
  • Spices (cumin, coriander, garam masala)
  • Oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Mix flour, salt, and oil to make the dough
  2. Boil and mash potatoes, then mix with cooked peas and spices
  3. Divide dough into small balls and roll into circles
  4. Cut each circle in half to form semi-circles
  5. Fold into cone shape and fill with potato mixture
  6. Seal the edges with water
  7. Heat oil in a deep pan
  8. Fry samosas until golden brown
  9. Drain on paper towels
  10. Serve hot with chutney
